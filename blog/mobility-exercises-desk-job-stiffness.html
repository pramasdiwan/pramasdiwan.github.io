Mobility

Mobility Exercises For Desk-Job Stiffness That Actually Help

5 min read Pramas Diwan

Eight to ten hours of sitting a day takes a measurable toll — tight hip flexors, rounded shoulders, and a lower back that aches by evening. Mobility work specifically targets these areas, undoing the effects of a sedentary day in just a few focused minutes.

Why Stretching Alone Isn't Enough

Static stretching lengthens a muscle temporarily, but mobility work — controlled movement through a joint's full range of motion — builds lasting flexibility and strength in that range. That's why a 10-minute mobility routine outperforms a quick stretch before bed.

A 10-Minute Desk-Recovery Routine

  • Hip flexor stretch — 30 seconds per side, releasing tightness from prolonged sitting
  • Cat-cow spinal mobility — 10 slow reps, restoring movement through a stiff lower back
  • Thread the needle — 30 seconds per side, opening up tight shoulders and upper back
  • 90/90 hip switches — 10 reps, restoring hip rotation lost to sitting
  • Wall shoulder slides — 10 reps, counteracting rounded, desk-hunched posture

When To Do This

First thing in the morning, or as a midday break between meetings, works best — ideally both. Even 5 minutes, done consistently, outperforms a single 30-minute mobility session done once a week.

The Bigger Picture

Mobility isn't separate from fat loss and strength goals — it's the foundation that lets you train safely and consistently. Stiff hips and a tight back limit how well you squat, lunge and move, which limits your results everywhere else.
